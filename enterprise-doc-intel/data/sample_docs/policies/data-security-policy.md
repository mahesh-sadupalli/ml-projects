# Data Security Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the standards and procedures for protecting organizational data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.

## Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who access company systems or data.

## Data Classification

### Confidential
- Customer personal data (PII)
- Financial records and projections
- Employee salary and HR records
- Trade secrets and proprietary algorithms
- Access: Restricted to authorized personnel only

### Internal
- Internal communications and meeting notes
- Project plans and roadmaps
- Non-public policies and procedures
- Access: Available to all employees

### Public
- Marketing materials
- Published documentation
- Press releases
- Access: No restrictions

## Access Control
- All systems require multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Access follows the principle of least privilege
- Access reviews conducted quarterly by IT Security team
- Service accounts require approval from the CISO
- Passwords must be at least 16 characters with complexity requirements

## Data Handling
- Confidential data must be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Data must not be stored on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
- Backup procedures follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite
- Data retention: 7 years for financial records, 3 years for operational data

## Incident Response
1. **Detection**: Report suspected breaches to security@company.com within 1 hour
2. **Containment**: IT Security isolates affected systems
3. **Investigation**: Forensic analysis within 24 hours
4. **Notification**: Affected parties notified within 72 hours per GDPR requirements
5. **Recovery**: Systems restored from verified clean backups
6. **Post-mortem**: Root cause analysis and preventive measures documented

## Third-Party Requirements
- Vendors must comply with SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001
- Data processing agreements (DPA) required before data sharing
- Annual security assessments of critical vendors

## Compliance
This policy aligns with GDPR, SOC 2, and ISO 27001 frameworks. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
